CASE STUDY 01
Green Vista
Brand Film · 2026
Client
Green Vista, Leppington NSW
Type
Masterplanned residential community
My role
Director · Producer · Cinematographer · Editor
Timeline
~3 weeks · concept to delivery

The Brief

A 120+ residence masterplanned community in Sydney's Southwest growth corridor, near the future Western Sydney Airport. The client needed a brand film that looked premium without an agency budget — and someone who could think about what to show, not just how to shoot.

My Approach

I started with the commercial fundamentals: who's actually buying, what they care about, what would emotionally trigger an enquiry. I built the film around three beats matched to the buyer's journey — arrival, space, belonging. Aesthetic in service of marketing logic, not separate from it.

CASE STUDY 02
AI Career
Platform
Beta Research · Under NDA
Client
Sydney-based AI startup (NDA)
Type
User research strategy & execution
My role
Research lead — strategy, instruments, interviews, synthesis
Timeline
~4 weeks

The Brief

An AI career platform preparing for beta launch needed to validate three things: does the product solve a real problem, who are the ideal users, and what needs to change before paid conversion. The founders needed someone to design the entire research framework — not just run a focus group.

My Approach

I built a multi-stage research pipeline from scratch — screening to onboarding to structured feedback to in-depth 1-on-1 interviews. I wrote all three research instruments, conducted user interviews directly, and synthesised three rounds of findings into product and UX recommendations.

What set this engagement apart wasn't just the methodology — it was the iteration. Interviews ran in rounds rather than a single fixed batch. After each round, I revised the question set and re-selected participants based on what we'd learned. The research design evolved with the findings, instead of being locked in at the start.

"Exactly what we needed to make decisions about our next phase."

— Founder · AI Career Platform (NDA)

CASE STUDY 02 · METHODOLOGY
Research as strategy, not service.
01 · Strategy

Mapped the research to real business decisions — willingness to pay, friction points, engagement versus drop-off — not generic product questions.

02 · Instruments

Designed three research instruments from scratch: screening survey, mid-experience questionnaire, and semi-structured interview guide.

03 · Iterative Rounds

6–10 carefully selected participants, run in iterative rounds. Each round informed the next round's questions and participant criteria — research as a living process, not a fixed script.

04 · Synthesis

Three rounds of synthesis, translating raw user voice into product insights and UX recommendations the team could act on this week.

05 · Reusability

The framework was built to be re-run — not used once. Future product phases can re-execute without rebuilding.

06 · Outcome

Persona refinements, friction points the analytics had missed, and a clearer path to the platform's next phase.

CASE STUDY 03
Boutique
Salon
Content Strategy & Production · 2025
Client
Sydney boutique hair salon (design-led)
Type
Content strategy + system design + production
My role
Strategy, creative direction, photography, video
Engagement
Foundation phase · delivered over 2 months

The Brief

A premium boutique salon with a strong in-person brand but almost no digital presence. Their content read like individual stylists' portfolios rather than one coherent voice — meaning if a key stylist left, the audience would go too. They needed a content approach that built something more durable.

My Approach

The problem wasn't too little content — it was content with no structure. I designed a three-pillar content system (Meet Our Stylists / Founder Recommends / Salon Stories) so the salon's content built one consistent identity, not scattered personal brands. I wrote the approach in both English and Chinese and produced the first round of videos and photography.

The Outcome

Delivered the founding content system: a bilingual content strategy (EN/CN), the three-pillar framework, and the initial signature assets — hero videos and photoshoots. I set it up so the salon's own team could keep running it, and they've continued posting on their own since.
